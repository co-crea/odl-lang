## 2. The Solution: ODL (Organizational Definition Language)

Current management is stuck in the **"ClickOps"** era. Processes are tightly coupled to the physical layer (specific humans).
We propose **ODL**: a language designed to bring the **Declarative Paradigm** to organizational orchestration.

### Source-IR-Kernel Architecture: Physics, Not Magic
ODL eliminates manual wiring. The user declares intents (Source) — such as `generate_team` or `approval_gate`.

1.  **Compiler:** Lowers intent into an optimal **Intermediate Representation (IR)** — an execution plan for thousands of agents.
2.  **Kernel:** A rigorous state machine (Analyzer, Planner, Runner) cycles continuously to execute the IR.

The Kernel controls probabilistic, fuzzy AI agents with strict logic.
> **"Running probabilistic AI on top of a deterministic substrate."**

## 4. Historical Context: The SQL Moment

This "scalability wall" is not unprecedented. Before 1970, programmers manually addressed physical sectors on magnetic tape, tightly coupling data and logic.

We were saved by the **invention of SQL**.

SQL abstracted "How" (physical retrieval) in favor of declaring "What" (desired predicates). ODL is the SQL moment for the Organization: separating the logical "Structure" from the physical "Workers."
